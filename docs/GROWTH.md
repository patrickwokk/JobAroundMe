# Growth — becoming the thing students check by reflex

RateMyProfessor won because it had data nobody else had, exactly when students needed it
(registration week). The equivalent play here:

## The moats (data nobody else has)

1. **The lab directory with recruiting signals.** No one — including the university — maintains a
   probed, cross-college index of professor lab sites. Every semester it stays fresh, the moat deepens.
2. **The freshness contract.** Handshake and department pages rot; this board provably sweeps dead
   listings. "Verified today" is the habit-forming stamp.
3. **Next: crowdsourced campus-job intel.** The RMP move itself — students reporting real pay,
   hours flexibility, and "would you recommend this job/lab" for on-campus positions. The
   [experience-report issue form](../.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/experience-report.yml) collects these
   at $0 today; when there are ~50 reports, render them on job/lab cards. Pay transparency for
   campus jobs would be genuinely unprecedented and extremely shareable.

## Seasonal spikes (when to push)

| When | Why | Move |
|---|---|---|
| Early August | Freshmen arrive, everyone needs a job | RSO fair presence, dorm QR flyers, GroupMe drops |
| Late September | Fall career fair panic | "Tracks" pages answer "what do I even apply to" |
| January | Summer-internship scramble | Referral drive on Connect |
| March–April | Labs plan fall rosters | "Email a professor this week" push + composer |

## Channels, cheapest first

- **Club partnerships.** Every club on `/clubs` has a GroupMe/Discord. Offer each officer a
  pre-filtered link (`/clubs?major=eecs`, `/tracks/big-tech-swe`) — they get content, we get reach.
- **Professors themselves.** Labs listed here get inbound students; email PIs a "your lab is
  listed, want edits?" note. Some will link back — .edu backlinks are SEO gold.
- **SEO.** Track and major pages target long-tail searches real students make ("UofA research
  assistant how", "internships near University of Arkansas"). Static pages + fast loads + real
  content is the whole playbook.
- **RSS + digest.** `/feed.xml` exists for power users now; a Buttondown-based weekly digest
  (free < 100 subscribers) is the retention layer when someone will run it.
- **Reddit/word of mouth.** One genuinely useful comment in r/uark threads during peak weeks
  beats any campaign. Never astroturf; the site's honesty is the brand.

## The metric that matters

Weekly returning visitors (GoatCounter). Job boards are habit products: if students don't come
back weekly, features don't matter. Target: 40% of visitors returning within 14 days during
semester. Below that, improve freshness and email; above it, pour on distribution.
